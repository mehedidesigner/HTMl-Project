ABOUT UNIVERSITY:

History
World University of Bangladesh (WUB)established in 2003 under the private University Act 1992 (amended in 1998) and private university Act 2010, approved and recognized by the Ministry of Education, Government of the People's Republic of Bangladesh and the University Grants Commission (UGC) of Bangladesh is a leading university for quality and utilitarian education. His Excellency the President of People’s Republic of Bangladesh is the Chancellor of the university. The WUB is a non-profit making concern - and governed by a 9 Member board of trustees constituted as per private universities Act1998 (Amended in 2010) .
Infra-structural facilities
The University is located in the heart of Dhaka city. The physical infra-structural facilities include spacious classroom, examination halls, teaching aid for various teaching pedagogy, library and lab facilities, online library, BDREN, facilities for games and sports and for co-curricular and extracurricular activities. Library
World University of Bangladesh has a large library in the present campus with maps and journal area, and a photocopy corner, database search corner, and newspaper display section. Each day around 950 users visit the library. The library contains 18663 books, a subscription to 135 international and national journals (hardcopy), 3 million online journals (Shared by IEEE Library) on its database, subscription to 16 national daily newspapers and around 2544 CD-ROMs and audio cassettes. It makes use of the UGC’s online facilities. Journals World University of Bangladesh has been publishing 4 Journals twice in a year regularly since 2010 with Volume I and Volume II, Chronicle, Magazines, Newsletter, Monographs etc. periodically to disseminate the research findings and activities of the university at National and International Levels. Names of our Journals given below: 1) World Journal of Business Studies (wjbs), 2) International Journal of Entrepreneurship and Development Studies, 3) World Journal of Science and Engineering (WJSE), 4) South Asian Journal of Population and Health,
Accommodation
The university provides residential facilities for male and female students.
Permanent campus
This year the World University of Bangladesh (WUB) inaugurates its state-of-art campus located in the newly urbanized area of the city that is the
Uttara Third Phase Sector 17.
This place has a striking waterfront setting, close to the lakes of the sector making it a congenial environment for academic purposes. The campus, being erected is a purpose-built building with capacity to house 12,000 students with more two phases for erection in the next five years. The new campus houses facilities such as indoor gym, basketball court, a helipad, interactive e-learning classrooms, and sophisticated Labs.
